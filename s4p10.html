National Oral Health Programme (NOHP)

Promoting Oral Health Equity Across India

Target Group (To Whom It Applies)

  • Children – for early prevention and hygiene awareness
  • Adolescents and Adults – for lifestyle-related oral health guidance
  • Elderly – prone to chronic and untreated oral issues
  • Pregnant Women & Mothers – maternal and infant oral care
  • Rural & underserved communities with limited access to dental services

Eligibility Criteria

  • Open to all Indian citizens through public health systems
  • Focus on inclusion of vulnerable and underserved groups
  • No income, caste, or geographic restriction for availing oral health services

Introduction to the Scheme

The National Oral Health Programme (NOHP) was launched in 2014–15 under the National Health Mission to tackle the growing burden of oral diseases in India. It is the first dedicated initiative to integrate oral healthcare into mainstream public health services.

What It Contains (Benefits Offered)

  • Free oral screening and treatment at government health facilities
  • Infrastructure strengthening at District Hospitals, CHCs, and PHCs
  • Setting up/upgrading Dental Care Units with essential equipment
  • Free IEC/BCC activities to promote brushing, hygiene, and anti-tobacco habits
  • Access to e-DantSeva, India’s first digital oral health platform
  • IVRS Helpline (1800-11-2032) for oral health queries

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW), Government of India
  • Implemented via: National Health Mission (NHM), with support from State and District Health Departments
  • Partners: Dental colleges, public health institutions, and NGOs

Application Process

  • No formal application is required
  • Free services are available at designated government healthcare facilities
  • Dental camps and outreach programs are also organized for rural populations

Duration & Timeline

  • Launched during 2014–15 under the NHM
  • Currently ongoing as a key part of India’s public health services

Pros & Limitations

Strengths:

  • First nationwide oral health programme in India
  • Improves public awareness and access to dental care
  • Supports digital empowerment through e-DantSeva

Weaknesses:

  • Dental infrastructure and manpower still limited in remote areas
  • Awareness and health-seeking behavior remain low in rural belts

Real-World Impact

  • Improved integration of oral healthcare into primary care
  • Increased availability of free dental services in public hospitals
  • Higher awareness around oral hygiene and risk factors like tobacco
  • School health initiatives incorporating oral health modules

BONUS: Strategic Insight

NOHP bridges a crucial gap by recognizing oral health as a vital part of overall health. By focusing on infrastructure, prevention, and education, the programme has the potential to reduce both health and economic burdens on vulnerable communities.
