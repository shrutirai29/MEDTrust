Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK) – DEIC

Ensuring Every Child Gets a Healthy Start in Life

Target Group (To Whom It Applies)

  • Children from birth to 18 years
  • Focus: 0–6 years (critical for early intervention)
  • Children with:
    • Defects at birth (e.g., congenital heart disease, cleft lip)
    • Developmental delays/disabilities (e.g., cerebral palsy, autism)
    • Diseases (e.g., rheumatic heart disease)
    • Deficiencies (e.g., malnutrition, vitamin deficiencies)

Eligibility Criteria

  • All children in India, regardless of income or location
  • Priority groups include:
    • Newborns from Sick Newborn Care Units (SNCUs)
    • Children screened by Mobile Health Teams
    • Self-referred or community-referred children (e.g., by ASHA workers)

Scheme Introduction

RBSK under DEIC aims for early identification and intervention of health conditions that can cause long-term disabilities. It supports NHM goals and aligns with India's commitment to the UN SDGs on health and education.

Scheme Details

  • Screening: 30+ conditions including vision/hearing defects, neuro-motor issues
  • Diagnosis: Confirmed at DEICs
  • Interventions:
    • Physio, speech, and occupational therapy
    • Referral for surgeries
    • Nutritional counseling and support
  • Follow-up: Linked to rehab and special education
  • Free services for all eligible children

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Central Government under NHM + State contribution
  • Implemented by: MoHFW, State Health Depts, and District Hospitals
  • Partners: Ministries of Social Justice, Education, Women & Child Development

Application Process

  • Referral via Mobile Health Teams, Anganwadis, schools, or self-referral
  • At DEIC:
    • Registration and clinical exam
    • Tests (e.g., hearing, vision)
    • Personalized intervention plan
  • Documents: Aadhaar, birth certificate, screening report (if any)

Duration & Timeline

  • Launched in 2013
  • Ongoing scheme with no specific deadlines

Pros & Limitations

Pros:

  • All-in-one center for child health and development
  • Free services reduce burden on families
  • Focus on crucial 0–6 years for best outcomes

Limitations:

  • Low awareness in rural areas
  • Shortage of pediatric specialists
  • Referral delays to higher care centers

Real-World Impact

  • 27+ million children screened annually (as per NHM)
  • Significant detection of congenital disorders
  • Challenges:
    • Lack of equipment/staff in some DEICs
    • Follow-up dropouts due to travel difficulties

Example: A 2-year-old child with speech delay is screened at an Anganwadi → referred to DEIC → diagnosed with hearing loss → receives hearing aids and speech therapy → linked to a special educator.

BONUS: Strategic Insight

RBSK, through DEICs, bridges preventive pediatric healthcare and developmental support. It not only improves early diagnosis but reduces long-term societal costs of untreated childhood conditions. Its holistic model—screening, intervention, and rehabilitation—offers a roadmap for integrated child health in India.
