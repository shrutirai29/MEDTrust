NPCDCS

National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, Cardiovascular Diseases & Stroke

Target Group (To Whom It Applies)

  • Adults aged 30 and above
  • Individuals with lifestyle-related risk factors (tobacco, alcohol, poor diet, inactivity)
  • Patients with diabetes, hypertension, or suspected cancer
  • Women (especially for breast and cervical cancer screening)
  • Rural and urban poor with limited access to private healthcare
  • Healthcare workers trained under the programme

Eligibility Criteria

  • No income, caste, or residence restrictions
  • Adults aged 30+ are eligible for routine screenings
  • Anyone at risk of or suffering from NCDs can access services at public health facilities

Introduction to the Scheme

Launched in 2010 under the National Health Mission (NHM), NPCDCS addresses the rising burden of non-communicable diseases (NCDs), responsible for nearly 60% of all deaths in India. The programme seeks to prevent, screen, diagnose, and manage major chronic illnesses such as diabetes, cardiovascular diseases, stroke, and cancer, with a strong emphasis on lifestyle modification and early detection.

What It Contains (Benefits Offered)

  • Free screening for diabetes, hypertension, and common cancers
  • Provision of essential medicines for NCDs at government facilities
  • Referral and follow-up services through a three-tiered healthcare system
  • Community-level awareness campaigns and lifestyle interventions
  • Training and capacity building for doctors, nurses, and frontline workers
  • Physiotherapy and counseling services at select centers

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India
  • Implemented by: State and District Health Missions under NHM
  • Fund Flow: Centrally sponsored with support through NHM flexi-pool

Application Process

  • No formal application is required
  • Citizens can walk into designated government health facilities for screening and treatment
  • ASHAs and ANMs conduct community-based screenings and referrals

Duration & Timeline

  • Launched in 2010 during the 11th Five Year Plan
  • Ongoing under the National Health Mission (NHM)
  • Expanded over the years to cover all states and union territories

Pros & Limitations

Strengths:

  • First nationwide initiative for chronic disease control
  • Widespread screening and diagnostic coverage
  • Integrated care and prevention strategy
  • Linkage with Ayushman Bharat HWCs enhances reach

Weaknesses:

  • Shortage of trained personnel in rural/remote areas
  • Gaps in drug supply and diagnostic availability
  • Difficulty in ensuring long-term follow-up and compliance
  • Digital data reporting inconsistencies in some regions

Real-World Impact

  • Over 10 crore people screened under population-based initiative
  • 677 District NCD Clinics, 187 Cardiac Care Units, 266 Day Care Centres established
  • Over 5392 CHC-level NCD clinics functional
  • Improved early detection rates and management of diabetes, cancer, and hypertension
  • Integration with Health and Wellness Centres for comprehensive care
