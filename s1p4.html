Universal Immunisation Programme (UIP)

A National Health Mission (NHM) Initiative

1. Target Group (To Whom It Applies)

  • Children: Newborns to 16 years of age.
  • Pregnant Women: For both maternal and child protection.

2. Eligibility Criteria

  • All children and pregnant women in India, regardless of background.
  • No documents or income proof required.
  • Completely free services at public facilities.

3. Introduction to the Scheme

  • Launched in 1985 as India’s largest public health program.
  • Focuses on preventing deadly childhood and maternal diseases.
  • Part of WHO’s Expanded Programme on Immunization (EPI).

4. What It Contains (Benefits Offered)

Vaccines for Children:

  • BCG, OPV, Hepatitis B, Pentavalent, Rotavirus, PCV
  • Measles-Rubella (MR), JE (in specific areas), IPV
  • DPT Boosters, Td

Vaccines for Pregnant Women: Td (Tetanus and Diphtheria)

All vaccines are free and available at government hospitals, health centers, and Anganwadis.

5. Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Central Govt. under NHM + support from WHO, UNICEF, Gavi, etc.
  • Implemented by: MoHFW, State Health Depts, District Officers, ASHA/ANM workers.

6. Application Process

  • No formal application needed.
  • Visit nearest PHC, CHC, Anganwadi, or attend Immunisation Day.
  • Receive an immunisation card after first dose.

7. Duration & Timeline

  • Ongoing since 1985.
  • No deadline – continuous enrolment.
  • Vaccination schedule: At birth, 6/10/14 weeks, 9 months, 16-24 months, 5 years, 10-16 years.

8. Pros & Limitations

Pros:

  • Free and wide-reaching – covers millions annually.
  • Essential for preventing deadly diseases.
  • Updated vaccines included regularly.

Limitations:

  • Lack of awareness in remote areas.
  • Vaccine hesitancy and myths in some communities.
  • Cold chain and infrastructure challenges.

9. Real-World Impact

  • Over 26 million infants and 30 million pregnant women targeted yearly.
  • Helped India become Polio-free in 2014.
  • Key role in reducing child mortality rates.
  • Infrastructure used for COVID-19 vaccine delivery.

Need help? Visit your nearest PHC, Anganwadi or check NHM Portal.

BONUS: Strategic Insights

  • The UIP plays a vital role in India’s health infrastructure by integrating with other programs like Mission Indradhanush and COVID-19 response.
  • Success depends on real-time data collection, digital immunisation tracking, and regular awareness drives.
  • Innovative use of cold chain logistics and digital tech (like CoWIN) has modernised delivery methods.
  • Partnerships with NGOs and local leaders are crucial to overcome myths and resistance in underserved areas.
