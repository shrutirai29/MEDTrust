Navjaat Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (NSSK)

Reducing Neonatal Mortality Through Comprehensive Newborn Care

1. Introduction

NSSK is a Government of India initiative aimed at reducing neonatal mortality and morbidity by training healthcare providers in essential newborn care and resuscitation. It is part of the RMNCH+A strategy under the National Health Mission (NHM).

2. Target Group

  • Newborns (0-28 days)
  • Doctors, nurses, and midwives involved in newborn care

3. Eligibility

  • Healthcare workers in delivery points, SNCUs, NSUs
  • Facilities with high delivery rates

4. Benefits

  • Hands-on training for:
    • Newborn resuscitation
    • Thermal protection
    • Breastfeeding initiation
    • Infection prevention
    • Identifying danger signs
  • Ensures at least one trained staff member per delivery point

5. Funding & Implementation

  • Funded by: Government of India under NHM
  • Implemented by: State/District Health Authorities
  • Training: Conducted at identified centers

6. Application

  • Healthcare workers nominated by facilities
  • Scheduled training sessions at district or state level
  • Mandatory completion of NSSK module

7. Timeline

  • Launched in 2009
  • Ongoing training at national and state levels

8. Pros & Limitations

Strengths:

  • Reduces neonatal mortality
  • Improves healthcare provider skills
  • Cost-effective and sustainable

Weaknesses:

  • Uneven training quality across regions
  • Needs frequent refresher sessions
  • Monitoring varies by state

9. Real-World Impact

  • Improved newborn survival in multiple states
  • Standardized care in delivery rooms
  • Boosted institutional delivery outcomes
