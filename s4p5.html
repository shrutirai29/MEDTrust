National Mental Health Programme (NMHP)

Accessible, Inclusive & Community-Based Mental Healthcare for All

Target Group (To Whom It Applies)

  • Individuals experiencing mental illness or psychological distress
  • Women, children, adolescents, elderly, and vulnerable populations
  • Families and caregivers needing psychosocial support
  • General public for awareness, stigma reduction, and early detection

Eligibility Criteria

  • Open to all Indian citizens regardless of income, caste, or location
  • No documentation or formal application required for Tele MANAS access
  • Walk-in access at District Mental Health Programme (DMHP) centers

Introduction to the Scheme

Launched in 1982, the National Mental Health Programme (NMHP) is India’s pioneering mental health initiative aimed at ensuring the availability and accessibility of mental health care for all. It promotes community-based services, integration with general healthcare, and uses technology like Tele MANAS to reach underserved regions. NMHP was strengthened through additions like the District Mental Health Programme (DMHP) in 1996 and the National Tele Mental Health Programme (NTMHP) in 2022.

What It Contains (Benefits Offered)

Core Benefits:

  • Free 24x7 mental health support via Tele MANAS helpline (14416)
  • Community-based mental health services under DMHP in 700+ districts
  • Integrated mental healthcare at primary, secondary, and tertiary levels
  • Training of general medical staff in basic mental health care

Support Services:

  • Psychiatric OPD and inpatient services at government hospitals
  • Awareness programs and IEC campaigns to reduce stigma
  • Linkages with education and social sectors for early detection

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India
  • Implemented by: State and District Health Departments
  • Technical Support: NIMHANS and medical colleges
  • Partners: NGOs, schools, and civil society organizations

Application Process

  • No formal application process needed for basic services
  • Individuals can walk in to district mental health units or call 14416
  • Follow-up care and referrals managed by health professionals

Duration & Timeline

  • NMHP launched in 1982
  • DMHP added in 1996 for decentralized implementation
  • NTMHP and Tele MANAS launched in 2022
  • Ongoing programme under National Health Mission

Pros & Limitations

Strengths:

  • India was among the first developing countries to adopt a national mental health policy
  • Widespread reach through DMHP and Tele MANAS services
  • Progressive legislation via Mental Healthcare Act, 2017
  • Focus on rights-based and community-oriented care

Weaknesses:

  • Severe shortage of trained mental health professionals
  • Infrastructural gaps in rural and tribal areas
  • Stigma and lack of awareness still hinder access
  • Limited inter-sectoral coordination in many states

Real-World Impact

NMHP has reached millions through community mental health clinics and helplines. The Tele MANAS platform has enabled 24/7 access to counseling across all states and UTs. The Mental Healthcare Act, 2017 has strengthened the legal framework for rights-based care, and training initiatives have expanded mental health capacity in general healthcare settings.

BONUS: Strategic Insight

NMHP's three-tier model—community-based services, integration with primary care, and digital outreach via Tele MANAS—offers a scalable framework to address India’s vast mental health burden. With proper investment in workforce and awareness, NMHP can transform mental health accessibility in India.
