National Programme for Control of Blindness & Visual Impairment (NPCBVI)

Eliminating Avoidable Blindness, Promoting Eye Health for All

Target Group (To Whom It Applies)

  • Individuals with visual impairment or blindness
  • School children with refractive errors
  • Elderly population with cataracts or age-related eye disorders
  • People with diabetes, hypertension, or glaucoma
  • The general population for preventive eye care awareness

Eligibility Criteria

  • No specific income, gender, or caste barrier
  • Services accessible via public health infrastructure
  • Special outreach for tribal, rural, and underserved communities

Introduction to the Scheme

Launched in 1976, NPCBVI is a centrally sponsored programme aimed at reducing avoidable blindness and improving visual health. Over the years, it has integrated new strategies such as early screening, treatment of eye conditions like cataract and glaucoma, and strengthening of healthcare infrastructure. The programme aims to bring down blindness prevalence to 0.3%.

What It Contains (Benefits Offered)

  • Free cataract surgeries and intraocular lens implantation
  • Free spectacles for children with refractive errors
  • Screening and treatment for diabetic retinopathy, glaucoma, and other eye diseases
  • Establishment of Vision Centers and Mobile Eye Units
  • Training of healthcare workers and ophthalmologists
  • Public awareness campaigns and community outreach drives

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare (MoHFW), Government of India
  • Implemented by: State and UT Health Departments, RIOs, District Hospitals, Medical Colleges
  • Partners: NGOs and private providers with support for service delivery and training

Application Process

  • Screenings conducted at schools, Vision Centers, and health facilities
  • No formal application required for services
  • Identified patients referred to appropriate facilities for free treatment

Duration & Timeline

  • Launched: 1976
  • Ongoing under National Health Mission (NHM)
  • Regular upgrades as per Five-Year Plans

Pros & Limitations

Strengths:

  • Nationwide coverage including remote areas
  • Strong public-private partnerships
  • Massive reach via school and community eye health programs

Weaknesses:

  • Shortage of trained ophthalmic professionals
  • Infrastructure gaps in rural and tribal regions
  • Low awareness among marginalized communities
  • High dependency on NGOs and voluntary support

Real-World Impact

  • Blindness prevalence reduced from 1.4% to ~1%
  • Set target of 0.3% blindness by 2020
  • Over 60 lakh cataract surgeries conducted annually
  • Thousands of school children receive free spectacles each year
  • Public eye health awareness significantly improved

BONUS: Strategic Insight

NPCBVI blends medical service delivery with strong community outreach and awareness. By expanding training and infrastructure and integrating with school and NCD programmes, India can lead global efforts in reducing avoidable blindness.
