National Programme for Prevention and Control of Fluorosis (NPPCF)

Fighting Fluorosis with Prevention, Education & Safe Water

Introduction

  • Launched in 2008–09 by the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW).
  • Aims to address fluorosis caused by excessive fluoride intake from water and food sources.

Objectives

  • Prevent and control dental, skeletal, and non-skeletal fluorosis.
  • Identify and manage fluorosis cases across affected areas.
  • Increase awareness about safe drinking water and balanced nutrition.

Coverage and Expansion

  • Initially started in 2008–09.
  • Currently active in 163 districts across 19 States/UTs.
  • Expanded to districts with high fluoride prevalence based on surveys.

Key Strategies

Surveillance

  • Fluoride surveys in schools, communities, and water sources.

Capacity Building

  • Training for medical officers, lab technicians, and healthcare workers.

Diagnostic Facilities

  • Testing fluoride in water and urine samples.
  • Diagnosis through clinical and radiological tools.

Management & Treatment

  • Medical treatment including calcium, vitamin C, and B-complex supplementation.
  • Rehabilitation and orthopedic surgical support in severe cases.

Health Education & IEC

  • Awareness campaigns on safe water usage and dietary changes.
  • Promoting defluoridation and alternative water sources.

Special Focus Areas

  • Water Monitoring: Regular testing and tracking of fluoride levels.
  • Safe Water Access: RO plants and community-level defluoridation systems.
  • Nutritional Interventions: Promoting diets that reduce fluoride absorption.

Implementation in Gujarat

  • Active in districts such as Jamnagar, Sabarkantha, Vadodara, and Banaskantha.

Vision for 2030

  • Ensure fluoride-safe drinking water for all.
  • Collaborate with Jal Shakti and other water ministries.
  • Significantly reduce fluorosis and waterborne disease burden in India.

Integrated Programmes

  • Jal Jeevan Mission
  • Swachh Bharat Abhiyan
  • National Programme for Prevention and Control of NCDs (NP-NCD)
  • National Rural Health Mission (NRHM)

Monitoring & Evaluation

  • Baseline and follow-up surveys in affected districts.
  • Performance reviews at district and state levels.
  • Monitoring impact on public health outcomes and quality of life.

Pros & Limitations

Strengths:

  • Focuses on both prevention and treatment of fluorosis.
  • Inter-sectoral coordination with water, nutrition, and health bodies.
  • Community awareness and behavioral change components included.

Weaknesses:

  • Limited awareness in remote regions.
  • Inconsistent implementation across states.
  • Challenges in regular fluoride monitoring in all districts.

Strategic Insight

The NPPCF stands at the intersection of environmental health and preventive medicine. By integrating water safety, nutrition, and diagnostics, it offers a holistic model to combat endemic fluorosis. Strengthening inter-sectoral collaboration can accelerate its success.
