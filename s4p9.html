National Programme for Prevention & Management of Burn Injuries (NPPMBI)

Comprehensive Burn Care: From Prevention to Rehabilitation

Target Group (To Whom It Applies)

  • Women in rural areas using open flames or kerosene stoves
  • Children at risk of fire accidents or scalding
  • Industrial workers in hazardous environments
  • Acid attack survivors
  • Healthcare professionals treating complex burn injuries
  • District Hospitals and Medical Colleges offering burn care services

Eligibility Criteria

  • Any person suffering from burn injuries seeking care at participating medical institutions
  • No discrimination based on age, gender, income, or location

Introduction to the Scheme

The National Programme for Prevention & Management of Burn Injuries (NPPMBI) was initiated as a pilot during the 11th Five Year Plan and formally launched on 6th February 2014 under the 12th Five Year Plan. It aims to reduce burn-related deaths and disabilities by creating a national infrastructure for prevention, emergency response, treatment, rehabilitation, and public awareness.

What It Contains (Benefits Offered)

Prevention & Awareness:

  • IEC/BCC campaigns for safety education in schools and communities
  • Demonstrations on emergency first-aid for burns

Treatment & Emergency Services:

  • Specialized burn units, ICUs, and operation theatres
  • Provision of skin grafting and reconstructive surgeries

Rehabilitation:

  • Counseling, physiotherapy, and prosthetics support
  • Reconstructive and plastic surgery for survivors

Training & Capacity Building:

  • Modular training programs for doctors and paramedics
  • Workshops on surgical techniques and infection control

Monitoring & Research:

  • Burn Injury Registry system for national surveillance
  • Studies on epidemiology and cost-effectiveness

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India
  • Implemented by: State Health Departments, National Health Mission (NHM)
  • Partners: Safdarjung Hospital, Dr. RML Hospital, CDC Atlanta, Central Design Bureau

Application Process

  • Burn patients can directly access care at designated public health facilities
  • Institutions are pre-approved by a screening committee based on infrastructure readiness
  • No formal application is required from patients

Pros & Limitations

Strengths:

  • Nationwide burn care infrastructure
  • Specialized services for both treatment and rehabilitation
  • Focus on vulnerable populations like women and acid attack victims
  • Robust monitoring system through Burn Injury Registry

Limitations:

  • Uneven implementation across states
  • Infrastructure gaps in rural and remote hospitals
  • Need for greater community-level awareness campaigns

Real-World Impact

  • Operational burn care units in over 50 Medical Colleges and 17 District Hospitals
  • Improved first-aid awareness and reduced burn mortality in intervention areas
  • Standardized burn care practices and protocols introduced nationwide
  • Enhanced rehabilitation and social reintegration of burn and acid attack survivors

BONUS: Strategic Insight

NPPMBI is a rare example of an integrated healthcare programme covering prevention to post-treatment rehabilitation. By investing in both infrastructure and education, it empowers the healthcare system to handle one of the most painful and disabling forms of trauma. Its replication across all states can make burn care accessible and effective for every Indian.
