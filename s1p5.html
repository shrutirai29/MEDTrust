Intensified Mission Indradhanush (IMI) 4.0

Under the Universal Immunization Programme – National Health Mission

1. Target Group

  • Primary Beneficiaries: Children under 2 years of age and pregnant women
  • Focus: Unvaccinated/partially vaccinated children, especially in underserved areas
  • Special Focus: Urban slums, nomads, migrants, brick kilns, tribal populations

2. Eligibility Criteria

  • Children: Aged 0–2 years who missed or partially received routine vaccines
  • Women: Pregnant women missing required immunizations
  • No income, caste, or religion criteria — based on immunization status

3. Scheme Introduction

  • Closes the immunization gap for children and pregnant women
  • Part of the Universal Immunization Programme (UIP)
  • Goal: Achieve 90% full immunization coverage

4. Scheme Details

  • Free immunization services for children and pregnant women
  • Catch-up rounds in low-coverage areas
  • Tracking of missed cases via micro-plans and due lists
  • Involvement of ASHAs, ANMs, AWWs for mobilization

5. Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Govt. of India
  • Implemented by: State & District Health Departments
  • Supported by: WHO, UNICEF, and other development partners

6. Application Process

  • No formal application required – beneficiaries are identified proactively
  • Health workers mobilize and track eligible individuals
  • Tools used: Micro-plans, due lists, special outreach sessions

7. Duration & Timeline

  • Launched: 2022
  • Phased implementation – e.g., Round 1 in Feb, Round 2 in March 2022
  • Status: Ongoing under the UIP

8. Pros & Limitations

  • Pros:
    • Focus on hard-to-reach populations
    • Effective use of frontline health workers
    • Data-driven identification of gaps
  • Limitations:
    • Over-dependence on field workers
    • Difficulty tracking mobile populations
    • Parental hesitancy or low awareness

9. Real-World Impact

  • Past phases (IMI 1.0 to 3.0) improved coverage by 6–7% in targeted districts
  • Challenges: COVID-19 disruptions, logistics in remote areas
  • Impact data for IMI 4.0 is still being assessed

BONUS: Strategic Insight

IMI 4.0 strengthens the Universal Immunization Programme by targeting the “last mile” — reaching children and mothers in hard-to-reach and underserved communities. With its data-driven micro-planning and focus on inclusivity, it sets a precedent for public health outreach in India.
