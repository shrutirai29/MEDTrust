National Tobacco Control Programme (NTCP)

Promoting Tobacco-Free Living for a Healthier Nation

Target Group (To Whom It Applies)

  • Tobacco users seeking cessation support
  • General population at risk of tobacco-related diseases
  • Children, adolescents, and youth (especially through school programs)
  • Health professionals, teachers, NGOs, law enforcement agencies
  • Government policymakers and administrative authorities

Eligibility Criteria

  • No formal eligibility restrictions
  • Anyone affected by tobacco use or involved in tobacco control efforts can benefit
  • Open to all states, districts, and institutions participating in tobacco control

Introduction to the Scheme

The National Tobacco Control Programme (NTCP) was launched in 2007–08 under the 11th Five-Year Plan to tackle the rising health burden of tobacco consumption. It supports enforcement of the Cigarettes and Other Tobacco Products Act (COTPA), 2003, aligns with the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control (FCTC), and aims to reduce tobacco use through public awareness, legislation, and cessation support. NTCP was further strengthened after the Global Adult Tobacco Survey (GATS) 2009–10 and now integrates with the National Health Mission (NHM).

What It Contains (Benefits Offered)

  • Free access to tobacco cessation services through district-level centers
  • Training and capacity building for healthcare workers, teachers, and NGOs
  • Public awareness and Information, Education & Communication (IEC) campaigns
  • School-based interventions for youth tobacco prevention
  • Enforcement and monitoring of tobacco control laws (COTPA, 2003)
  • Community-based implementation through Panchayati Raj Institutions
  • Multi-level coordination (National, State, District)

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India
  • Implemented through: National, State, and District Tobacco Control Cells (NTCC, STCC, DTCC)
  • Support Structure: Integrated under the National Health Mission (NHM)

Application Process

  • No formal application needed for individuals seeking help
  • District Tobacco Cessation Centers offer walk-in services
  • States and districts implement NTCP based on government protocols

Duration & Timeline

  • Launched: 2007–08 (11th Five-Year Plan)
  • Expanded: Under the 12th FYP post-GATS-1 findings
  • Current Coverage: All 36 States/UTs, over 612 districts
  • Ongoing: As part of NHM tobacco control initiatives

Pros & Limitations

Strengths:

  • Nationwide implementation with legal and institutional backing
  • Multi-sectoral collaboration for awareness and enforcement
  • Reduced tobacco usage trends as per GATS-2 (8.1 million fewer users)

Challenges:

  • Poor enforcement in rural and peri-urban areas
  • Deep-rooted social acceptance of tobacco in some communities
  • Inconsistent availability of cessation services
  • Coordination gaps across departments
  • Opposition from informal tobacco industry and lobbyists

Real-World Impact

NTCP has been successful in expanding awareness and services across India. GATS-2 reported a significant drop in tobacco use. Districts now have functional cessation services, and public education on tobacco hazards has increased. Yet, stronger enforcement and service delivery are required to reach underserved populations and further reduce tobacco dependency.

BONUS: Strategic Insight

NTCP is a vital initiative combining law enforcement, medical support, and community education. With proper execution and coordination, it can drastically reduce India’s tobacco burden and related health costs. Greater integration with primary health and school systems will help build a sustainable tobacco-free culture.
