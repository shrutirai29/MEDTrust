MAA Programme

Mothers’ Absolute Affection – Promoting Breastfeeding & Child Health

Introduction to the Scheme

The MAA Programme was launched in August 2016 by the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare under the National Health Mission. It aims to promote breastfeeding and improve infant and young child feeding practices to reduce malnutrition and child mortality in India.

Objectives

  • Promote early initiation of breastfeeding within 1 hour of birth
  • Ensure exclusive breastfeeding for the first 6 months
  • Encourage continued breastfeeding up to 2 years or beyond
  • Introduce complementary feeding from 6 months onwards
  • Improve child nutrition and reduce infant mortality

Target Group

  • Pregnant women and lactating mothers
  • Newborns and infants up to 2 years old
  • Health workers including ASHAs, ANMs, and staff nurses

Key Focus Areas

  • Early Initiation of Breastfeeding: Providing colostrum as the baby’s first immunity booster
  • Exclusive Breastfeeding (0–6 months): No other food or drink is needed besides breast milk
  • Complementary Feeding: Start age-appropriate solids at 6 months while continuing breastfeeding

Implementation Strategy

  • Implemented through NHM using existing health infrastructure
  • Focus on counselling at labor rooms, postnatal wards, and home visits
  • Frontline workers trained to support breastfeeding and nutrition counselling

Components & Support

  • Community Awareness: IEC/BCC campaigns targeting mothers and caregivers
  • Interpersonal Counselling: Through ASHAs during ANC and PNC visits
  • Skilled Support: At delivery points by trained staff
  • Monitoring & Evaluation: Tracking indicators related to breastfeeding and nutrition

Benefits Offered

For Infants:

  • Strengthens immunity and reduces infections
  • Enhances cognitive development
  • Decreases risk of malnutrition

For Mothers:

  • Lowers risk of breast and ovarian cancers
  • Helps in natural birth spacing
  • Faster postpartum recovery

Programme Tools & Integration

  • Training modules for healthcare providers
  • Mother Support Groups and job aids
  • BFHI (Baby-Friendly Hospital Initiative) promotion
  • Integrated with schemes like Poshan Abhiyaan, JSY, JSSK, LaQshya
  • Supports SDG 2 (Zero Hunger) and SDG 3 (Good Health & Well-being)

Challenges & Way Forward

Challenges:

  • Cultural myths and family pressure against exclusive breastfeeding
  • Gaps in counselling quality and coverage
  • Lack of awareness in rural areas

Way Forward:

  • Enhance digital tools and mobile outreach
  • Strengthen community-based counselling support
  • Expand coverage of BFHI-certified facilities
  • Continue skill-building among frontline workers

Real-World Impact

The MAA Programme has contributed to improved breastfeeding rates across several Indian states. Increased awareness through frontline health workers and campaigns has helped in early initiation and exclusive breastfeeding practices, leading to better infant health outcomes.

BONUS: Strategic Insight

MAA strengthens the first line of defense for a newborn – breast milk. By combining counseling, community engagement, and institutional practices, the programme plays a critical role in India’s fight against infant mortality and malnutrition.
