National Tuberculosis Elimination Program (NTEP)

Eliminating TB in India by 2025 – Ahead of the Global Target

Introduction to the Scheme

The National Tuberculosis Elimination Program (NTEP), formerly known as RNTCP, is India's flagship public health initiative aimed at eliminating tuberculosis as a public health problem by 2025.

Target Group (To Whom It Applies)

  • All individuals showing signs and symptoms of TB
  • High-risk populations such as people living with HIV/AIDS
  • People in contact with TB patients
  • Healthcare workers and populations in overcrowded or low-income areas

Eligibility Criteria

  • All Indian citizens suspected or diagnosed with TB are eligible for free services under NTEP
  • No age, gender, or income bar for treatment

What It Contains (Benefits Offered)

  • Free TB diagnosis through microscopy and CB-NAAT
  • Free anti-TB drugs (including MDR/XDR-TB regimens)
  • Directly Observed Treatment (DOT) support
  • Nutritional and financial support through Nikshay Poshan Yojana
  • Digital tracking and patient follow-up

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funding: Jointly funded by the Government of India and global partners (e.g., WHO, USAID, Global Fund)
  • Implementing Agency: Central TB Division under the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, with support from state and district TB cells

Application Process

  • No formal application required by patients
  • Patients can visit any government health facility, PHC, CHC, or DOT center
  • Healthcare workers also conduct active case finding in high-risk areas

Duration & Timeline

  • Launched as RNTCP in 1997 and rebranded as NTEP in 2020
  • Goal: TB elimination in India by 2025
  • Standard treatment duration: 6–24 months depending on TB type

Pros & Limitations

Pros

  • Free treatment and diagnostics across India
  • Digital case tracking ensures better outcomes
  • High outreach with community involvement and public-private partnerships

Limitations

  • Drug-resistant TB remains a major challenge
  • Stigma still affects treatment-seeking behavior
  • Inconsistent quality of care in some private sectors

Real-World Impact

  • Millions of lives saved through early diagnosis and free treatment
  • TB notification and treatment success rates have improved steadily
  • Increased awareness and reduction in TB incidence in urban and rural areas

Timeline

  • Launched as RNTCP: 1997
  • Renamed to NTEP: 2020

Aim of the Program

To eliminate tuberculosis (TB) in India by 2025, five years ahead of the global target of 2030 set by the WHO.

Institutional Structure

  • Central TB Division (Ministry of Health & Family Welfare)
  • State TB Cells and District TB Centers
  • Tuberculosis Units (TUs) at sub-district level
  • Designated Microscopy Centres (DMCs)

Diagnostics & Treatment

  • Microscopy and CB-NAAT labs for accurate and early diagnosis
  • Free anti-TB medication, including regimens for drug-resistant TB
  • Nikshay: Digital portal for real-time TB case tracking and management

Major Challenges Tackled

  • TB-HIV co-infection management
  • Drug resistance (MDR-TB, XDR-TB)
  • Fragmented care in the private sector
  • Addressing stigma and improving treatment adherence

National Strategic Plan (2017–2025)

Four Pillars of Strategy:

  • Detect: Early and accurate diagnosis using advanced tools
  • Treat: Patient-centered care and adherence support
  • Prevent: Contact tracing, BCG vaccination, and preventive therapy
  • Build: Strengthen systems, engage communities, expand partnerships

Digital Support

  • Nikshay portal facilitates registration, follow-up, and treatment outcome tracking
  • Improves accountability and data-driven decision making
