National Iodine Deficiency Disorders Control Programme (NIDDCP)

Combating IDDs through Universal Salt Iodization & Public Health Action

Background

  • Iodine is essential for thyroid function and brain development.
  • Deficiency causes disorders like goitre, brain damage, cretinism, and impaired development.
  • IDDs remain a public health problem in many parts of India, especially Himalayan and tribal regions.

Evolution of the Programme

  • Started in 1962 as the National Goitre Control Programme (NGCP)
  • Renamed to NIDDCP in 1992 to address the full range of IDDs
  • Implemented by the Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India

Target Population

  • General population in iodine-deficient areas
  • Particularly vulnerable: pregnant women, infants, and children

Objectives

  • Assess the extent of IDDs across regions
  • Ensure universal consumption of iodized salt (≥15 ppm)
  • Regularly monitor salt and urinary iodine levels
  • Conduct awareness campaigns (IEC) on the importance of iodine

Key Components

a. Universal Salt Iodization (USI)

  • Mandatory iodization of all edible salt
  • Collaboration with Salt Commissioner, FSSAI, and manufacturers

b. Surveillance and Monitoring

  • Salt testing kits and urinary iodine analysis
  • Support from ICMR, State IDD Cells, and educational institutions

c. Legislation & Regulation

  • Ban on sale of non-iodized salt since 1997
  • Enforced by FSSAI and Food Safety Officers

Achievements

  • Over 93% households use adequately iodized salt (NFHS-5)
  • Marked reduction in visible goitre and neurological IDDs
  • Formation of State IDD Cells and strengthening of monitoring labs

Challenges

  • Ensuring quality and iodine content in salt during production & distribution
  • Weak enforcement in remote or rural regions
  • Low public awareness, especially in underserved communities
  • Non-compliance by small-scale salt producers

Future Strategies

  • Strengthen enforcement and salt testing systems
  • Promote coordination among MoHFW, FSSAI, Salt Commissioner
  • Enhance public outreach campaigns for awareness
  • Incorporate new technologies for salt fortification and IDD detection
  • Ultimate Goal: Elimination of IDDs as a public health issue

Implementing & Supporting Agencies

  • Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW)
  • Salt Commissioner’s Office (Ministry of Commerce)
  • Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI)
  • Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR)
  • UNICEF, WHO, and other international partners

BONUS: Public Health Insight

NIDDCP highlights the importance of preventive nutrition strategies. Iodized salt is one of the simplest, most cost-effective interventions to improve population health and cognitive potential across generations.
