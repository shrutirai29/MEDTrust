National Iron Plus Initiative (NIPI)

Combating Anaemia Across All Life Stages Through Iron & Folic Acid Supplementation

Target Group (To Whom It Applies)

  • Children aged 6–59 months
  • Children aged 5–10 years
  • Adolescents (10–19 years)
  • Pregnant and lactating women
  • Women of reproductive age (15–49 years)

Eligibility Criteria

  • Universal eligibility within the identified age groups
  • No income or caste restrictions
  • Accessible via schools, Anganwadi centers, and public healthcare institutions

Introduction to the Scheme

The National Iron Plus Initiative (NIPI) was launched under the National Health Mission (NHM) to address iron deficiency anaemia in India. It targets critical age groups with a life-cycle approach, offering Iron and Folic Acid (IFA) supplements and deworming tablets through schools, Anganwadi centers, and health facilities.

What It Contains (Benefits Offered)

Supplementation by Age Group:

  • 6–59 months: IFA syrup (20mg iron + 100mcg folic acid) biweekly
  • 5–10 years: IFA tablet (45mg iron + 400mcg folic acid) weekly
  • 10–19 years: IFA tablet (60mg iron + 500mcg folic acid) weekly
  • Pregnant women: Daily IFA tablet during pregnancy and 180 days post-partum
  • Lactating women: Daily IFA tablet for at least 6 months
  • Women of reproductive age: Weekly IFA tablet year-round

Deworming:

  • Albendazole administered twice a year on National Deworming Days

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India
  • Implemented by: State Health Departments via schools, Anganwadi centers, ASHAs, ANMs
  • Partnerships: Ministries of Education & Women and Child Development

Application Process

  • No application required
  • Supplements provided automatically at schools, Anganwadis, and health centers
  • Community-level delivery by ASHAs and ANMs

Duration & Timeline

  • Launched in 2013 under NHM
  • Integrated into Anaemia Mukt Bharat (2018)
  • Ongoing program aligned with SDG targets for 2030

Pros & Limitations

Strengths:

  • Targets anaemia at multiple life stages
  • Free and accessible supplements
  • Integrated with deworming and health education

Weaknesses:

  • Adherence issues, especially among adolescents
  • Stock shortages in some areas
  • Side effects like nausea affect regular intake
  • Awareness gaps in remote communities

Real-World Impact

NIPI has contributed to improved haemoglobin levels and reduced anaemia in various groups, especially school-going children and pregnant women. It is a critical component of the Anaemia Mukt Bharat strategy, aiming for a 3% annual reduction in anaemia prevalence.
