LaQshya Programme

Labour Room Quality Improvement Initiative

Target Group (To Whom It Applies)

  • All pregnant women and newborns in medical colleges, district hospitals, FRUs, and CHCs with over 100 deliveries/month.
  • Special focus on areas with fewer than 100 deliveries per month (e.g., hills/deserts).

Eligibility Criteria

  • Pregnant women and newborns in designated hospitals and CHCs as per the program's target list.
  • No income or caste-based restrictions.

Introduction to the Programme

The LaQshya programme, launched in 2017 by the Ministry of Health & Family Welfare (MoHFW), aims to improve quality of care in labour rooms, operation theatres, and obstetric ICUs/HDUs. The initiative focuses on improving maternal and newborn care through infrastructure upgrades, training, and adherence to clinical protocols.

What It Contains (Benefits Offered)

For Pregnant Women:

  • Improved quality of care during the labour and immediate postpartum period
  • Respectful maternity care (RMC)
  • Access to essential obstetric equipment and medicines
  • Improved infrastructure at hospitals and CHCs

For Newborns:

  • Enhanced neonatal care in Obstetric ICUs and HDUs
  • Access to essential newborn care, including resuscitation and infection control

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India
  • Implemented by: State Health Missions & District Health Societies
  • Fund Flow: Directly managed by the Ministry through the NHM

Application Process

  • No formal application is needed for beneficiaries.
  • Participating facilities must meet LaQshya standards to qualify for certification and incentives.
  • Implementation is managed at the institutional level with support from state and district health authorities.

Duration & Timeline

  • Launched in December 2017.
  • Ongoing programme under NHM, with periodic evaluation and updates.

Pros & Limitations

Strengths:

  • Improved infrastructure and quality of care in critical maternity services.
  • Reduction in maternal and newborn mortality rates in participating hospitals.
  • Provision of skilled staff and comprehensive training.

Weaknesses:

  • Uneven implementation across states and regions.
  • Challenges with resource allocation in rural or under-resourced areas.
  • Limited reach in some remote locations with less than 100 deliveries per month.

Real-World Impact

The LaQshya programme has contributed to better maternal and neonatal health outcomes in medical colleges, district hospitals, and CHCs by enhancing healthcare infrastructure, improving training, and ensuring the availability of essential equipment. Despite challenges in some areas, the programme has played a significant role in improving the quality of maternity care across India.

BONUS: Strategic Insight

LaQshya exemplifies the importance of quality assurance in maternal health services. Its focus on upgrading healthcare facilities and training staff has been crucial in improving healthcare delivery, ensuring that women and newborns receive the best possible care during critical times.

Certification, Incentives & Branding

  • Certification: Based on NQAS Standards
    • Platinum: ≥90%
    • Gold: ≥80%
    • Silver: ≥70%
  • Incentives:
    • ₹6 lakh – Medical Colleges
    • ₹3 lakh – District Hospitals
    • ₹2 lakh – FRUs
  • Requires >80% satisfaction & performance on key indicators

Way Forward

  • Scale maternal-newborn innovations to more facilities
  • Enhance patient experience and public trust
  • Stronger coaching and monitoring systems
  • Support SDG targets for maternal and child health
  • End all preventable maternal and newborn deaths
