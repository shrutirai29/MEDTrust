Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA)

Under the National Health Mission (NHM)

Target Group

  • All pregnant women in their second and third trimesters.
  • Especially those at risk due to anemia, high blood pressure, malnutrition, etc.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Must be pregnant and in the 2nd or 3rd trimester.
  • No income, caste, or religion restrictions.
  • Services are available on the 9th of every month at government health facilities.

Introduction to the Scheme

Launched by the Ministry of Health & Family Welfare in 2016, PMSMA aims to provide free, high-quality antenatal care to pregnant women in their second and third trimesters. It helps in early risk detection, promotes institutional deliveries, and supports safe motherhood practices under the NHM umbrella.

What It Contains (Benefits Offered)

  • Free ANC (Antenatal Check-ups) by doctors on the 9th of every month.
  • Includes:
    • Physical examination
    • Lab tests (Hb, sugar, urine)
    • Blood pressure check
    • Iron, calcium tablets
    • Tetanus injections
  • Focus on high-risk pregnancy detection and birth planning.
  • Private doctors can volunteer via the PMSMA mobile app.

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare (Central Government)
  • Implemented through: State and district health departments
  • Supported by: ASHAs, ANMs, Anganwadi Workers, PHCs, CHCs, District Hospitals

Application Process

  • No formal application needed
  • Visit a government health center on the 9th of any month
  • SMS registration available for pregnant women (5616115)
  • Doctors can volunteer via the PMSMA mobile app

Pros & Limitations

Strengths:

  • Promotes regular antenatal checkups
  • Encourages private doctor participation in public healthcare
  • Free, comprehensive care and risk detection

Weaknesses:

  • Doctor availability varies, especially in rural areas
  • Awareness issues may reduce turnout
  • Urban slums and remote areas may remain underserved

Real-World Impact

The scheme has led to a rise in antenatal visits in public health institutions and improved early detection of pregnancy-related complications. However, private doctor engagement and follow-up mechanisms still need improvement in many regions.

BONUS: Strategic Insight

PMSMA leverages a fixed monthly schedule to deliver preventive maternal care services. By mobilizing both public and private medical professionals, it addresses systemic gaps in antenatal care and risk management. If scaled effectively, it can be a cornerstone for reducing maternal mortality in India.
