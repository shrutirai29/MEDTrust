Why Health Matters

It’s not just about avoiding sickness — real health means being strong physically, feeling mentally stable, staying emotionally balanced, and having good relationships with others. When people are healthy, they can learn better, work well, deal with stress, and live happier lives. A healthy population also helps the country grow by contributing in positive ways.
In today’s busy world, where many people face stress and lifestyle-related diseases, taking care of health is more important than ever. Regular checkups, eating healthy food, exercising, and looking after mental health can help prevent bigger problems. It’s also important to know about available health services and government health schemes. Understanding these can help people take better care of themselves and their families.
At MEDTrust, we believe health is a basic right for everyone — not just a privilege for a few. Everyone should have access to good, affordable healthcare and reliable information. By learning more and taking action, we can build healthier families, stronger communities, and a better future for all.

Government Policies Supporting Health

The Indian government has started many health programs and schemes to make sure that everyone in the country can get good healthcare, no matter where they live or how much money they earn. These programs focus on helping people stay healthy, preventing serious diseases, and improving the overall health system. The goal is to reduce health problems among poor and vulnerable groups, support mothers and children, and build strong hospitals and clinics in both cities and villages.
Some of these policies provide free or low-cost health insurance to families who can't afford expensive treatments. Others help in creating digital health records so that doctors can easily access a patient's medical history. The government also works to prevent diseases before they happen by encouraging regular checkups, vaccinations, and spreading awareness about hygiene, nutrition, and mental well-being
Special attention is given to health education campaigns, immunization drives for children, nutritional programs for pregnant women and kids, and mental health support. These efforts help people understand the importance of health and how to take care of themselves and their loved ones.
But many people still don’t know about these helpful government schemes or how to use them. That’s where MEDTrust comes in. We want to make health policies easier to understand and access. Our goal is to connect people with the right information at the right time, so they can use these services and take control of their health.
When people are informed and supported, they can live better lives and make healthier choices. With the help of government policies and platforms like MEDTrust, we can create a healthier, stronger, and more aware India — one person at a time.

Programs Run by Government under National Health Mission

Reproductive, Maternal, Neonatal, Child and Adolescent Health (RMNCH+A)

This program ensures healthcare support for women, newborns, children, and adolescents at every stage of life. Below are key initiatives launched under this category:

Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakaram (JSSK) Rashtriya Kishor Swasthya Karyakram (RKSK) Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK) Universal Immunisation Programme Mission Indradhanush (MI) Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY) Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA) Navjaat Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (NSSK) National Programme for Family Planning LaQshya Programme (Labour Room Quality Improvement)

National Nutritional Programmes

These programs aim to improve nutrition levels among children, pregnant women, and lactating mothers through interventions like the Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) and Mid-Day Meal schemes. They focus on reducing malnutrition and promoting healthy growth.

National Iodine Deficiency Disorders Control Programme RMAA (Mothers’ Absolute Affection) Programme for Infant and Young Child Feeding National Programme for Prevention and Control of Fluorosis (NPPCF) National Iron Plus Initiative for Anaemia Control

Communicable Diseases

Under NHM, the government runs multiple initiatives to control and eliminate infectious diseases such as tuberculosis, malaria, HIV/AIDS, and vector-borne diseases. These include awareness campaigns, free treatment, and national surveillance programs.

Integrated Disease Surveillance Programme (IDSP) Revised National Tuberculosis Control Programme (RNTCP) National Leprosy Eradication Programme (NLEP) National Vector Borne Disease Control Programme (NVBDCP) National AIDS Control Programme (NACP) Pulse Polio Programme National Viral Hepatitis Control Program (NVHCP) National Rabies Control Programme National Programme on Containment of Anti-Microbial Resistance (AMR)

Non-Communicable Diseases

Programs under this category address long-term health issues like diabetes, cancer, hypertension, and heart disease. The focus is on early screening, lifestyle change, regular monitoring, and free access to essential medicines and care.

National Tobacco Control Programme(NTCP) National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, Cardiovascular Diseases & Stroke (NPCDCS) National Programme for Control Treatment of Occupational Diseases National Programme for Prevention and Control of Deafness (NPPCD) National Mental Health Programme National Programme for Control of Blindness & Visual Impairment (NPCB&VI) Pradhan Mantri National Dialysis Programme (PMNDP) National Programme for the Health Care for the Elderly (NPHCE) National Programme for Prevention & Management of Burn Injuries (NPPMBI) National Oral Health programme
