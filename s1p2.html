Rashtriya Kishor Swasthya Karyakram (RKSK)

Empowering Adolescents with Knowledge, Support & Health Services

Target Group (To Whom It Applies)

  • Adolescents aged 10 to 19 years
  • Boys and girls from rural and urban areas
  • School-going, out-of-school, married, and unmarried adolescents

Introduction to the Scheme

Launched in 2014, RKSK is Indiaâ€™s flagship adolescent health programme under the National Health Mission. It addresses physical, mental, emotional, and social needs of adolescents by promoting healthy behavior and preventive care through community and school-based interventions.

What It Contains (Benefits Offered)

Health Services:

  • Nutrition education and Weekly Iron Folic Supplementation (WIFS)
  • Mental health counseling for stress, anxiety, and depression
  • Sexual and reproductive health education and guidance
  • Screening for non-communicable diseases (NCDs)
  • Substance abuse prevention and awareness
  • Support related to gender-based violence, injuries, and safety

Delivery Platforms:

  • Peer Educators (Saathiyas)
  • Adolescent Friendly Health Clinics (AFHCs)
  • School-based awareness sessions
  • Adolescent Health and Wellness Days

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India
  • Implemented by: State Health Departments via NHM
  • Execution: Through health workers, schools, peer educators, and NGOs at district/block levels

Application Process

  • No formal application required
  • Adolescents can visit AFHCs or participate in village/school health sessions
  • Programs are announced locally through schools and community workers

Duration & Timeline

  • Launched in 2014
  • Ongoing as a key adolescent health initiative under NHM
  • Regular updates and expansions based on health needs

Pros & Limitations

Strengths:

  • Addresses holistic health needsâ€”physical, mental, emotional
  • Peer-led education empowers youth from within communities
  • Targets both school-going and out-of-school adolescents

Weaknesses:

  • Peer educators may lack proper training or remuneration
  • Inconsistent quality of counseling services across regions
  • Awareness and outreach still low in tribal and rural areas

Real-World Impact

RKSK has helped bridge the gap in adolescent healthcare through community engagement and accessible counseling services. Adolescents are more informed about menstrual hygiene, nutrition, and reproductive health in many areas. However, continued investment and awareness are essential for deep rural penetration.

  • Over 1.5 lakh Peer Educators trained
  • AFHCs established in PHCs and CHCs across states
  • Positive behavioral changes reported in WIFS and hygiene
