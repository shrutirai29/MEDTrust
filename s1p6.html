Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY)

Under the National Health Mission (NHM)

Target Group

  • Poor pregnant women from rural or economically weaker sections
  • Focus on Low-Performing States (LPS) with fewer institutional deliveries

Eligibility Criteria

  • Pregnant women who are BPL or belong to SC/ST categories
  • In LPS: All pregnant women aged 19+ delivering in government/accredited facilities
  • In High-Performing States: Only BPL and SC/ST women are eligible

Scheme Introduction

  • Encourages safe institutional deliveries over home births
  • Launched in 2005 under NRHM, now part of NHM
  • Addresses high maternal and newborn death rates in India

Benefits Offered

  • Cash assistance for women delivering in govt/accredited hospitals
  • Support for transportation, delivery, and post-natal care
  • ASHAs help identify, guide, and support the mothers
  • Assistance amount varies by state and rural/urban status

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • 100% Centrally Sponsored by the Ministry of Health & Family Welfare
  • Implemented by State & District Health Departments under NHM
  • ASHAs act as the community link workers

Application Process

  • No formal application needed from the beneficiary
  • ASHAs/health staff identify and help the pregnant woman
  • Required documents: Pregnancy registration, BPL card, ID proof
  • Payments made directly via bank or health facility

Duration & Timeline

  • Launched: 2005
  • Status: Ongoing and active under NHM

Pros & Limitations

Strengths:

  • Boosted institutional deliveries across India
  • Financial aid for safe childbirth in hospitals
  • Strong outreach by ASHA workers

Weaknesses:

  • Payment delays in some areas
  • Shortage of facilities in rural hospitals
  • Traditional birth preferences persist in some communities

Real-World Impact

  • Sharp rise in institutional deliveries since 2005
  • Reduction in Maternal Mortality Ratio (MMR) and infant deaths
  • Challenges: Delay in funds, unequal implementation, and follow-up tracking
