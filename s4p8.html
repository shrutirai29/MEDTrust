National Programme for Health Care of the Elderly (NPHCE)

Ensuring Dignified Aging Through Accessible, Specialized Care

Target Group (To Whom It Applies)

  • Elderly individuals aged 60 years and above
  • Senior citizens requiring preventive, promotive, curative, and rehabilitative healthcare

Eligibility Criteria

  • All Indian citizens aged 60 and above
  • Access available via public health institutions across levels — SCs, PHCs, CHCs, District Hospitals, and Medical Colleges
  • No income, caste, or residence-based restrictions

Introduction to the Scheme

Launched in 2010–11, the NPHCE aims to provide comprehensive and dedicated healthcare services to India’s rapidly aging population. With elderly numbers projected to surpass 300 million by 2051, the programme provides integrated care for the elderly under both primary and tertiary systems through NHM and Rashtriya Varisth Jan Swasthya Yojana.

What It Contains (Benefits Offered)

  • Free health services for elderly at public facilities
  • Weekly/bi-weekly geriatric clinics at PHCs & CHCs
  • Home-based elderly care and physiotherapy
  • Dedicated 10-bed geriatric wards at District Hospitals
  • 30-bed specialized geriatric centers at selected medical colleges (RGCs)
  • Multidisciplinary care for complex conditions like dementia, falls, etc. at National Centres for Ageing

Funding & Implementing Agency

  • Funded by: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India
  • Implemented by: State Health Missions under NHM
  • Flexi-pool support: NHM and Rashtriya Varisth Jan Swasthya Yojana

Application Process

  • No formal application required
  • Services can be accessed directly at participating public healthcare facilities
  • Protocols and service packages standardized by central and state health authorities

Duration & Timeline

  • Launched in 2010–11
  • Currently an ongoing scheme under NHM and tertiary care expansion

Program Structure

  • Primary & Secondary Care (NHM): Services via Sub-Centres, PHCs, CHCs, and District Hospitals
  • Tertiary Care (Rashtriya Varisth Jan Swasthya Yojana):
    • 19 Regional Geriatric Centres (RGCs) in medical colleges
    • 2 National Centres for Ageing (NCAs): AIIMS Delhi and MMC Chennai

Service Package Overview

Health Facility Services Offered
Sub-Centres Home-based care, health education, support group referral
PHCs Weekly geriatric OPDs, medicines, basic screening
CHCs Bi-weekly clinics, rehab units, physiotherapy, home visits
District Hospitals 10-bed geriatric wards, diagnostic and lab services
RGCs 30-bed geriatric inpatient care, training, research
NCAs Specialty clinics (falls, memory, dementia), national protocol development

Key Strategies

  • Community-based elderly care and outreach services
  • Dedicated geriatric clinics and physiotherapy units
  • Home visits for bed-bound and frail elders
  • Training modules and PG courses in Geriatric Medicine
  • Mass awareness through IEC material in multiple languages
  • Collaboration with research bodies (e.g., LASI) for impact assessment

Real-World Impact

  • Implemented in over 713 districts of India
  • Services offered through 584 District Hospitals, 3111 CHCs, and 10180 PHCs
  • In 2019–20 alone:
    • 2.65 crore OPD visits
    • 9.68 lakh indoor admissions
    • 15.33 lakh physiotherapy sessions
    • 90.71 lakh lab investigations
    • 21.19 lakh elderly issued health cards
    • 2.4 lakh+ received home-based care

Human Resource Training

  • Training materials developed for medical officers, nurses, and community workers
  • Network of 85 National Master Trainers and 236 State-level Trainers under ToT model

LASI Project Integration

The Longitudinal Ageing Study in India (LASI) is a key evidence-generation tool for the NPHCE. Its first wave (2017–18) surveyed over 72,000 adults, including 31,000+ elderly, capturing insights into:

  • Health: Morbidity, disability, chronic illness
  • Healthcare Access: Cost, coverage, insurance
  • Social Dynamics: Family support, isolation, mental well-being
  • Financial Security: Income, pensions, employment post-retirement

Digital Presence and IEC

The official portal https://nphce.nhp.gov.in provides key documents, updates, and data on implementation.

IEC content — including videos, leaflets, and awareness posters — is regularly disseminated in multiple languages to ensure senior citizens across India are informed and empowered.
